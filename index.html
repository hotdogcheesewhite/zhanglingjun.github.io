Welcome to my homepage! 👋

I am a Master's student at Tsinghua University, working at the intersection of end-to-end autonomous driving, vision-language models (VLM), and multimodal reasoning. My current research explores how large multimodal models can perceive, reason about, and act in complex physical-world scenes — with a special emphasis on driving world models, long-horizon planning, and formal traffic-law compliance.

Previously, my work focused on the formalization and online monitoring of traffic right-of-way laws for autonomous vehicles at urban intersections, published at IEEE IV 2025. I have since extended my research to driving world models (DeepSight, ICML 2026) and progressive multimodal reasoning for autonomous driving (MindDriver, CVPR 2026).

I am always open to discussions and collaborations — feel free to reach out!

Research Interests

End-to-End Autonomous Driving

Unified architectures that fuse perception, prediction and planning, evaluated on closed-loop benchmarks such as Bench2Drive.

Vision-Language Models

Adapting general-domain VLMs for embodied driving tasks with chain-of-thought, scene-grounded reasoning, and reinforcement fine-tuning.

Driving World Models

Predicting latent semantic states of future frames in BEV space to enable long-horizon planning and counterfactual rollouts.

Safety & Traffic Law Formalization

Translating ambiguous traffic regulations into machine-interpretable logical frameworks for online compliance monitoring.

DeepSight: Long-Horizon World Modeling via Latent States Prediction for End-to-End Autonomous Driving

Lingjun Zhang, Changjie Wu, Linzhe Shi, Jiangyang Li, Jiaxin Liu, Lei Yang, Hang Zhang, Mu Xu, Hong Wang

Proceedings of the 43rd International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026.

We propose a driving world model that performs parallel prediction of latent semantic features for consecutive future frames in the bird's-eye-view (BEV) space, enabling long-horizon modeling of future world states. We further introduce an efficient, adaptive text-reasoning mechanism that injects social knowledge for challenging long-tail driving scenarios, achieving state-of-the-art results on the closed-loop Bench2Drive benchmark.

MindDriver: Introducing Progressive Multimodal Reasoning for Autonomous Driving

Yujian Yuan*, Lingjun Zhang*, Changjie Wu, Xinyuan Chang, Xin Cai, Shuang Zeng, Linzhe Shi, Sijin Wang, Hang Zhang, Mu Xu

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2026.

MindDriver is a progressive multimodal reasoning framework that lets VLMs imitate human-like progressive thinking for autonomous driving — semantic understanding, semantic-to-physical imagination, and physical-space trajectory planning. With a feedback-guided data annotation pipeline and progressive reinforcement fine-tuning, MindDriver achieves superior performance on both nuScenes open-loop and Bench2Drive closed-loop evaluation.

Formalization and Online Monitoring of Right-of-way Laws for Autonomous Vehicles at Intersections

Lingjun Zhang, Chengxiang Zhao, Lei Yang, Lei Song, Ziying Song, Wenhao Yu

2025 IEEE Intelligent Vehicles Symposium (IV), Cluj-Napoca, Romania, 22–25 June 2025.

We formalize right-of-way laws at urban intersections into a machine-interpretable logical framework and build a threshold analysis framework on top of the SIND dataset, rigorously defining the prioritization of right-of-way. The optimal compliance threshold is determined via sensitivity analysis under the proposed Weighted TPN score (WTPNs) and deployed for online intersection monitoring.
